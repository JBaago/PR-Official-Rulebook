1.1 Overview

The Progression Riders (PR) Series is a multi-stage community race series consisting of 8 stages. Each season rewards tactical riding, teamwork, and consistency across multiple terrain profiles.

1.2 Groups

  • C Group ‚Äî expect lead beacon at 3.2 W/kg
  • B Group ‚Äî expect lead beacon at 4.0 W/kg
  • The early phase includes the Chase Pot ‚Äî a dynamic inter-group bonus (see Section 2).

1.3 Rider Categories & Adjustments

  • B riders in C Group: earn 50% of awarded points.
  • A riders in B Group: earn 75% of awarded points.
  • Riders may switch groups mid-season or mid-ride.
    • Points earned in one group apply only to that group‚Äôs leaderboard.
    • Points cannot be transferred between groups.
  • If a rider does not finish a stage, any points already earned remain valid.
  • Rider category is determined post ride for the individual stage.

1.4 Jersey Classifications (by Terrain / Segment Type)

Three jerseys are contested, each linked to the type of segment (terrain or profile):

Red ‚Äî Hilly / Climbing segments (KOMs, punchy)
Green ‚Äî Flat / Sprint segments
Yellow ‚Äî Finish lines, longer climbs & rouleur segments
The method of point allocation (FTS or FAL) is defined per segment in the Stage Brief and announced on the ride (see Section 3). A combined leaderboard merges B and C Group results for each jersey classification.

1.5 Stage Profiles

  • Puncheur ‚Äî Hilly; mix of Red & Green segments.
  • Grimpeur ‚Äî Mountain; favors Red & Yellow segments.
  • Rouleur ‚Äî Long steady efforts; favors Yellow segments.
  • Sprinteur ‚Äî Flat; favors Green segments.

1.6 Zero-Point Stage Rule (ZPS) ‚Äî Mandatory

  • Each rider must designate two ZPS per season.
  • During a ZPS, all points are void, regardless of participation.
  • Non-attendance: automatically counts as a ZPS.
  • Participation: ride normally but forfeit points (e.g., to support others or form alliances).
  • Deadline: Declare latest within 5 minutes of stage start.
  • Irrevocable: Declarations cannot be revoked.
  • Compliance: If two ZPS are not used by season end, the final one or two stages automatically count as ZPS.
  • Communication: ZPS may be submitted privately to the stage leadership (cross-group secrecy allowed). There‚Äôs no limit to how early a stage can be declared a ZPS. No obligation to announce to other riders.

1.7 Communication & Results

All stage plans, segment details, and results are published via the PR Series official channels (website, Discord, or designated social posts).

2 ‚Äî Groups & the Chase Pot

‚ñ∂

2.1 Group Start Order

  • C Group starts 3 minutes ahead.
  • B Group starts 3 minutes behind and begins with a chase of C Group.
  • Each group has its own Yellow Beacon responsible for pacing and coordination.
  • Every stage begins with a mandatory 5-minute warm-up, ramping gradually each minute before the official start.

2.2 The Chase Pot Concept & Tactical Dynamics

The Chase Pot is a bonus pool for the B Group based on how early they catch C.

  • C Group goal: cooperate to hold off the catch as long as possible, to diminish B‚Äôs Chase Pot.
  • B Group goal: cooperate to catch efficiently and maximize the pot value with a minimum of Decay Points.

2.3 Pot Value & Decay

  • Initial pot = sum of all segment points C Group has contested before the catch.
  • After the Decay Point, the pot reduces by 25% per segment or route markers as announced.
  • Final value depends on catch timing relative to each decay marker.
  • Each stage‚Äôs Decay Point, segment order, and pot type are published in the Stage Brief.

2.4 Catch Definition & Beacon Rules

  • A catch is only valid once the two Yellow Beacons (B & C) are joined in the same visible group.
  • Mixed riders alone do not constitute a catch.
  • Beacons must stay with their own group and not ride off front or drop behind independently.
  • Riders are encouraged to pull their respective group, keeping the Beacon protected in the draft.
  • Beacon power caps apply from the very beginning of the ride (including warm-up):
    • C Beacon cap: average 3.2 W/kg
    • B Beacon cap: average 4.0 W/kg

2.5 Chase Pot Distribution

When the catch occurs (beacons joined), the final Chase Pot is divided equally among the B-Group riders who score points in the subsequent relevant segments, aligned with the type of segments C Group contested during the Chase (üü• Red, üü© Green, üü® Yellow). Riders on ZPS are excluded.
